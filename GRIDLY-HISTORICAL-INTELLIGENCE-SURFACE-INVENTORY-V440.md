# V440 — Historical Intelligence Surface Inventory & Readiness Audit

## 1. Quick Summary

V440 is an audit-only inventory of Gridly historical-intelligence systems. No production behavior, UI, API, read path, DriveTexas state, Route Watch behavior, Supabase permissions, alert-card behavior, Awareness Brief behavior, Community Pulse behavior, or capture pipeline behavior was changed.

### Key discoveries

- Gridly has **five distinct historical system families**:
  1. Passive Historical Capture System.
  2. Historical Intelligence Engine.
  3. Historical Projection System.
  4. Historical Awareness Adapter.
  5. Historical Intelligence Sheet / legacy consumer sheet system.
- V429–V439-approved consumer-safe historical awareness is limited to the **Historical Awareness Adapter** and its secondary supporting lines on:
  - Awareness Brief.
  - Community Pulse.
  - Alert Cards.
- The codebase also contains a separate **Historical Intelligence Sheet** reachable through the Portrait V2 bottom dock sheet registry as `history`. That sheet is not owned by the Historical Awareness Adapter; it is generated by in-app historical intelligence sheet builders that read local historical-intelligence storage snapshots and render compact pattern rows.
- Browser-callable historical audits exist for passive collection, canary controls, writer diagnostics, projection, parity, historical intelligence, final rendering, and adapter output validation.
- Protected boundaries remain documented as the required state for this milestone:
  - `historicalReadsEnabled: false`
  - `historyUiEnabled: false`
  - `historicalApiExposure: false`
  - `consumerFacingHistoryDashboard: false`
  - `DriveTexasPaused: true`

#### Adapter ownership boundaries

- Owns only **consumer-safe transformation and display validation** of already-generated internal historical intelligence.
- Does **not** own raw historical storage, capture writes, projection generation, Historical Intelligence Sheet rendering, dashboard exposure, API exposure, or DriveTexas behavior.
- Approved language model is intentionally low-claim and non-predictive, e.g.:
  - “Repeated reports have been observed here.”
  - “Community reports have occurred here before.”
  - “Previous community reports have been observed nearby.”
  - “Historical evidence is still limited.”
- Safe-display controls suppress prohibited, predictive, raw-history, database/schema/table, user scoring, confidence-score, and low-evidence unsafe language.

## 4. Historical Intelligence Sheet Assessment

### Ownership

The Historical Intelligence Sheet originates from **Other / Historical Intelligence Sheet system**, with model input from the **Historical Intelligence Engine-style in-app findings builders**. It does **not** originate from the Historical Awareness Adapter and does **not** use adapter safe-display methods as its render owner.

### Current generation path

1. Portrait V2 registers a `history` sheet with title **“Historical Intelligence”** and HTML builder `buildGridlyHistoricalIntelligenceSheetHtml`.
2. `buildGridlyHistoricalIntelligenceSheetHtml()` calls `buildGridlyIntelligencePreviewCardModel()`.
3. The model uses local historical intelligence storage snapshots and `gridlyBuildHistoricalIntelligenceFindings()` outputs.
4. The sheet renders compact `<details>` rows for ranked/deduped findings, including category, row title, pattern subtitle, summary line, detail stats, and supporting copy.
5. `gridlyHistoricalIntelligenceFinalRenderAudit()` can inspect live DOM rows or rendered HTML rows for final-copy readiness.

### Current language examples

The sheet can emit language patterns such as:

- Empty state:
  - “Local patterns from cleared community reports”
  - “Not Enough History Yet”
  - “Gridly will show local patterns here after more cleared reports are collected.”
- Pattern rows / stats:
  - “Most reported”
  - “Typical delay”
  - “Usually clears”
  - “Reported by”
  - “Community reports”
  - “Last reported”
- Finding-derived copy may include examples matching previously observed language such as:
  - “Recurring road issue reported here”
  - “Most reported 6 PM–8 PM”
  - “Usually clears in 1 hr”

### Current source

- Source: local historical-intelligence storage snapshots and derived findings in `js/app.js`.
- Not sourced from `gridlyHistoricalAwarenessAdapter.SAFE_MESSAGES`.
- Not projection-owned based on the discovered render path.
- Not capture-writer-owned; capture/writer systems are storage/diagnostic sidecars, not sheet renderers.

### Current visibility status

- **Potentially visible / accessible** because it is registered in the Portrait V2 sheet template map as `history`.
- **Not classified as V439-approved consumer-safe historical exposure** because it is separate from the adapter-approved Awareness Brief, Community Pulse, and Alert Card lines.
- **Recommended status:** keep unchanged for V440; require explicit future decision before changing visibility, language, affordance, or data source.

## 5. Risk Assessment

### Overall risk: Medium

Rationale:

- **Low behavior risk for V440 itself** because this milestone is documentation-only and does not modify runtime behavior.
- **Medium product/exposure risk** because the Historical Intelligence Sheet appears potentially user-accessible and uses richer historical analytics language than the adapter-approved secondary awareness copy.
- **Low current API/read risk** based on discovered protected boundaries and audit posture; no new API exposure or historical read enablement was added.
- **Medium maintenance risk** because historical functionality now spans multiple owners: capture, engine, projection, adapter, sheet rendering, lifecycle/close audits, and map/layer ownership audits.

### Specific risks

| Risk | Level | Notes |
|---|---:|---|
| Adapter-approved surfaces overclaim historical meaning | Low | Adapter has prohibited language, raw-history, low-evidence, and prediction suppression. |
| Historical Intelligence Sheet bypasses adapter language model | Medium | Sheet copy is generated outside adapter safe-display controls. |
| Browser-callable audits confuse production posture | Low / Medium | Dev-console access exists, but not normal consumer UI. Naming and ownership should be documented. |
| Projection system accidentally treated as production truth | Low | Projection appears audit/shadow-oriented; future work should keep it isolated. |
| Passive capture writer/canary accidentally activated | Low / Medium | Guard checks exist, but canary is browser-callable and should remain restricted to internal validation. |
| Historical map/layer ambiguity | Unknown / Medium | Audit references exist; visible layer behavior was not fully traced in this milestone. |

## 6. Recommended Next Milestone

Recommended: **V441 — Historical Intelligence Sheet Ownership & Exposure Decision Gate**

Scope should remain audit/planning only unless separately authorized. Suggested objectives:

1. Decide whether the Historical Intelligence Sheet is:
   - removed from consumer navigation,
   - retained as hidden/internal only,
   - renamed/reframed as a limited awareness-support surface,
   - or promoted later behind explicit safe-display controls.
2. Require any future visible sheet language to pass an adapter-equivalent safety model:
   - no prediction/forecast language,
   - no raw counts/timestamps/user data unless explicitly approved,
   - low-evidence suppression/caveat,
   - secondary awareness framing,
   - no route-decision authority.
3. Add a focused map/layer historical exposure audit to resolve the current “unknown” map/layer references.
4. Keep DriveTexas paused and keep all protected boundaries unchanged.
