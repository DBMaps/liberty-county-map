# GRIDLY San Jacinto County Readiness Fast-Track Assessment V700

## 1. Executive Summary

V700 is a documentation-only fast-track assessment of San Jacinto County as a potential future county candidate for the Gridly County Platform Foundation. The assessment applies the V550 County Readiness Fast-Track Framework to determine whether San Jacinto County should be treated as a validated candidate for future governed planning.

San Jacinto County is a northern and northeastern regional neighbor to the current Liberty County-centered foundation area. It provides a rural, lake-adjacent, corridor-connected, and small-community planning context that differs from the Montgomery County readiness program and the Chambers County fast-track assessment. Its candidate value is primarily in testing whether the county platform can evaluate a less urbanized county with meaningful regional movement, informal community awareness patterns, and adjacency to both Liberty County and Montgomery County without creating activation pressure.

Scope is limited to planning-level readiness assessment. V700 does not create implementation authority, activation authority, onboarding authority, package authority, registry authority, storage authority, fixture authority, migration authority, Supabase authority, historical-read authority, DriveTexas authority, or Transportation Intelligence authority.

Final determination preview: **VALIDATED COUNTY CANDIDATE WITH OBSERVATIONS**. San Jacinto County appears suitable for future governed planning, but future work must explicitly preserve rural-awareness discipline, source-backed boundary and community validation, and cross-county corridor containment before any implementation milestone is considered.

## 2. Framework Basis

This assessment is based on the following prior milestones and lessons:

- **V550 County Readiness Fast-Track Framework.** V700 follows the V550 compressed assessment model, including candidate suitability, geographic and boundary readiness, transportation and corridor readiness, awareness and community-structure readiness, data-source and asset readiness, county-package readiness, onboarding readiness, risk evaluation, protected-boundary confirmation, and final determination logic.
- **V459 through V475 County Platform Foundation.** V700 preserves the activation architecture, Liberty normalization, county registry contract, storage namespace, read/write containment, package fixture, evidence, governance, dry-run, execution, and consolidation expectations established by the County Platform Foundation.
- **V500 through V507 Montgomery County Readiness Program.** V700 reuses the Montgomery program's lessons about adjacency, cross-county containment, county-package planning, evidence-first readiness, governance-first onboarding, and protected-boundary discipline.
- **V600 Chambers County Readiness Fast-Track Assessment.** V700 carries forward the fast-track lesson that a county may be validated for future planning only when observations remain explicit and are not treated as activation, onboarding, package, registry, storage, or runtime authority.

## 4. Category Assessments

### A. Candidate Suitability

- Score: **HIGH READINESS**
- Strategic value:
  - San Jacinto County provides a useful future-candidate profile because it is smaller, more rural, and more lake- and corridor-influenced than Montgomery County.
  - It can test whether the county platform's foundation can reason about rural county readiness without assuming urban or suburban operating patterns.
  - It extends the candidate sequence beyond the immediate Liberty-to-Montgomery and Liberty-to-Chambers patterns while remaining regionally coherent.
- Regional value:
  - San Jacinto County sits within a broader East Texas and Houston-region periphery where rural travel, lake-adjacent awareness, and north-south corridor movement may matter for future planning.
  - It can help evaluate whether regional movement observations can remain planning-level without enabling Transportation Intelligence.
- Expansion value:
  - The county provides a practical candidate for future low-to-moderate complexity package planning.
  - It may support future platform learning around small municipalities, unincorporated communities, rural roads, and county-edge containment.
- Liberty adjacency value:
  - San Jacinto County has direct adjacency relevance to Liberty County, making it useful for future containment review along a northern Liberty edge.
  - It also has planning relevance to Montgomery County, creating a useful triangulation point among Liberty, Montgomery, and San Jacinto without implying package creation.
- Observations:
  - Candidate value depends on future source-backed validation of boundaries, communities, and corridor assets.
  - Rural suitability should not be treated as lower effort; rural awareness can require careful handling of informal places and road-based identity.
- Risks:
  - The county's lower population density could tempt reviewers to under-scope governance and evidence requirements.
  - Regional corridor relevance could be overstated if not source-backed later.
- Future gates:
  - Future candidate planning must pass County Platform Foundation evidence, containment, package, registry, storage, governance, dry-run, and activation gates before any implementation work.

### B. Geographic & Boundary Readiness

- Score: **HIGH READINESS**
- County boundary suitability:
  - San Jacinto County has a clear county identity suitable for future boundary-based planning.
  - Future authoritative boundary geometry should be obtainable through ordinary county, state, or federal source categories, subject to later evidence acceptance.
- County containment suitability:
  - The county appears suitable for future county-scoped containment analysis because its formal county identity can support package, registry, storage, fixture, and read/write boundary planning.
  - Future containment rules must distinguish San Jacinto-owned records from Liberty, Montgomery, Polk, Walker, and other neighboring-county contexts.
- Boundary complexity:
  - Boundary complexity appears manageable but not trivial because edge roads, rural properties, lake-adjacent areas, and informal community references may cross or blur county identity.
  - Any future map geometry must use accepted authoritative boundaries rather than inferred road, postal, or community assumptions.
- Liberty interaction:
  - The San Jacinto-Liberty edge is useful for future northern Liberty containment validation.
  - Reports, roads, or awareness labels near the county edge must be assigned only through explicit county ownership rules.
- Montgomery interaction:
  - San Jacinto County's relationship to Montgomery County is useful for future regional-planning continuity after the Montgomery readiness program.
  - Future review should prevent Montgomery corridor or commuter context from overriding San Jacinto county containment.
- Observations:
  - Lake-adjacent and rural edge contexts should be treated as boundary-sensitive.
  - Future boundary assets must be independently accepted before any package or runtime assumption.
- Risks:
  - Informal place names, postal areas, and lake-area identities could create ownership ambiguity.
  - Cross-county road segments could be misclassified without geometry-backed containment.
- Future gates:
  - Authoritative boundary source selection, boundary freshness review, geometry acceptance, containment tests, and dry-run validation remain future-only.

- Rural movement patterns:

- Corridor complexity:
  - Corridor complexity is moderate because major routes are identifiable but future asset mapping must handle rural segments, edge transitions, and non-urban travel patterns carefully.
- Observations:

## 8. Final Determination

Final determination: **VALIDATED COUNTY CANDIDATE WITH OBSERVATIONS**

Rationale:

- San Jacinto County has strong candidate suitability due to Liberty adjacency, Montgomery interaction, rural East Texas planning value, and a coherent county identity.
- Geographic and county-package readiness are high at a planning level because future boundary-based containment and package identity appear feasible.
- Transportation, awareness, data-source, asset, and onboarding readiness are moderate because future source-backed work is required for rural corridors, informal community labels, lake-adjacent awareness, asset freshness, and implementation governance.
- No low-readiness category creates a blocker to candidate validation.
- Compatibility with V459 through V475 appears positive at a planning level.
- Protected historical, DriveTexas, and Transportation Intelligence boundaries remain closed.

Meaning: San Jacinto County is suitable for future governed planning only. This determination does not authorize implementation, activation, onboarding, package creation, registry entry creation, storage namespace creation, fixture creation, migration work, Supabase changes, historical-read exposure, DriveTexas resumption, or Transportation Intelligence.
