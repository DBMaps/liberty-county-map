# Gridly Community Reporting Disclaimer

## Purpose

Gridly uses community participation to improve local awareness. Reports help users understand what others have observed, but they are not guarantees about current conditions.

## Reports Are Community-Generated

Gridly reports may be submitted by community members using the app. The person submitting a report may not have complete information, and Gridly may not independently verify every submission before it appears in the awareness experience.

## Conditions May Change

Roads, crossings, hazards, traffic, weather, closures, and other local conditions can change quickly. A report that was accurate when submitted may become outdated by the time another user sees it.

## Reports Are Evidence, Not Guarantees

Community reports should be treated as evidence and awareness signals. They can help users notice possible issues, but they do not guarantee that a crossing is blocked, a road is clear, a hazard remains present, or a delay will occur.

## Verify Conditions When Safe

Users should verify conditions through direct observation, posted signs, official instructions, and other reliable sources when it is safe and lawful to do so. Users should never stop, slow suddenly, trespass, enter restricted areas, or use a device unsafely to confirm a report.

## Not Emergency Services

Gridly is not an emergency service and should not be used to request urgent help. In an emergency, users should contact local emergency services through the appropriate emergency number.

## Not Official Government Information

Gridly is not an official government, railroad, law-enforcement, emergency-management, or transportation agency source. Official instructions, posted closures, traffic control devices, and emergency directions always take priority over Gridly content.

## Community Participation Model

Gridly's trust philosophy is based on shared awareness, careful participation, and clear expectations. Community reports are most useful when users submit timely, accurate, and safe observations without exaggeration or personal information.

The goal is to help the community know before they go while preserving safety, humility, and transparency about what community-generated information can and cannot prove.
