# Gridly Privacy Policy

**Effective date:** [Insert effective date]

## Introduction

Gridly is an awareness platform that helps people understand reported conditions near their route, destination, and surrounding community before they travel. This Privacy Policy explains what information Gridly collects, how that information is used, and the choices available to users.

This draft is intended to support App Store and Google Play review preparation. It should be reviewed by qualified legal counsel before publication.

## Information Collected

Gridly is designed to collect only the information needed to provide local awareness, reporting, and route-watch context. Depending on how a user interacts with Gridly, the app may process:

- Approximate device location when the user grants location permission.
- Destination or route-watch search inputs entered by the user.
- Community report details submitted by the user, such as report type, nearby location, notes, timestamps, and related map context.
- App settings and locally stored preferences used to preserve the user experience.
- Technical information needed to operate, secure, synchronize, and improve the service.

Gridly does not use background location tracking as part of the current app distribution package.

## Location Usage

Gridly may request location access to show nearby awareness information and help users understand conditions around their current area. Location information is used to support awareness features such as nearby reports, crossing context, road hazards, and route-watch relevance.

Location access is optional and controlled by the user's device settings. If a user declines location access, some nearby-awareness features may be limited, but the app may still provide information through map browsing, destination search, or other available workflows.

Gridly is not a turn-by-turn navigation application and does not provide directional routing instructions.

## Community Reports

Users may submit community reports about local conditions such as blocked crossings, road hazards, closures, or other awareness events. Community reports may include user-entered details and location context so that other users can understand what was reported and where it may be relevant.

Community reports are intended to improve shared situational awareness. They may be visible to other Gridly users and may be synchronized through Gridly's backend systems.

Users should not include private, sensitive, identifying, or unsafe information in report notes.

## Local Storage Usage

Gridly may use local device storage to preserve app preferences, report history, cached awareness data, route-watch context, install experience state, and other non-sensitive operational data. Local storage helps Gridly remain responsive and maintain continuity between sessions.

Users may clear local app data through browser or device settings, though doing so may reset preferences or cached app state.

## Supabase Usage

Gridly uses Supabase as part of its backend synchronization infrastructure. Supabase may process community reports and related operational records needed to make shared awareness information available across devices.

Data synchronized through Supabase is used to support Gridly's awareness, reporting, and route-watch workflows. Gridly does not use Supabase synchronization to add background location tracking, push notifications, alternate route generation, or turn-by-turn navigation.

## Data Sharing

Gridly may share or make available information in the following limited ways:

- Community reports may be displayed to other users as shared awareness information.
- Service providers, including backend infrastructure providers, may process data on Gridly's behalf to operate and secure the app.
- Information may be disclosed if required to comply with law, protect rights, prevent abuse, or maintain service integrity.

Gridly does not sell personal information as part of the current app distribution package.

## User Rights

Depending on applicable law and where a user lives, users may have rights to request access, correction, deletion, portability, or restriction of certain personal information. Users may also control device permissions, including location access, through their operating system settings.

Requests will be evaluated according to applicable law, service requirements, safety considerations, and the nature of community-generated records.
