# LP038.3 Consumer Weather Authority Migration

## Prior consumer weather owners

Audit first found the visible weather paths in `js/app.js`: Awareness Brief/weather hero used `gridlyBriefInteractionWeatherBridge()` and candidate arrays from presentation globals, connector records, and provider records; Community Pulse/evidence promotion used `gridlyStoryWeatherEvidence()`; Travel Brief used `gridlyBuildTravelBriefModel()` and `gridlyTravelBriefWeatherLines()`; weather count copy was indirectly derived from the selected bridge model; quiet weather text came from Travel Brief and hidden/visible weather hero behavior; county, community, Houston parent, and Houston child summaries inherited the selected awareness area only through the bridge locality audit. These owners could produce independent raw or connector-retained weather interpretations.

## Migrated consumer weather owners

LP038.3 makes `gridlySelectConsumerVisibleWeatherSituations()` the shared consumer selector. It derives from `gridlyGetWeatherAuthoritySnapshot()` and the LP038.1 canonical authority result. Consumer-visible counts now represent unique authority-eligible weather situations, not raw NWS provider record counts or connector-retained record counts.

## Shared consumer selector

`gridlySelectConsumerVisibleWeatherSituations()` exposes the selected awareness area, active county, active community, authority status, consumer-visible situations, consumer-visible situation count, unique situation count, quiet-state reason and consumer meaning, source availability, freshness status, ownership/fallback methods observed, Current Conditions availability, Forecast availability, alerts availability, and fallback-disclosure status. It does not fetch, poll, write storage, mutate source records, or re-run connector filtering.

## Awareness Brief migration

The Awareness Brief weather bridge now asks the shared consumer selector first. If authority-eligible weather situations exist, the bridge returns an alert model owned by the authority selector. Existing legacy candidates remain as compatibility fallback only when the authority selector is unavailable or has no consumer-visible situation.

## Community Pulse migration

Community Pulse weather evidence continues through `gridlyStoryWeatherEvidence()`, which consumes `gridlyBriefInteractionWeatherBridge()`. Because the bridge now prefers authority-owned weather, Community Pulse no longer independently owns locality or count decisions when the LP038.3 selector is available.

## Travel Brief migration

Travel Brief continues to use the existing weather-line renderer, but the weather model comes from the authority-first bridge. DriveTexas ownership and roadway-line behavior were not changed.

## Alert panel migration

LP038.3 documents alert-panel weather ownership as migrated where the existing weather model feeds visible weather rows. Non-weather rows remain untouched: community reports, crossings, DriveTexas, hazards, Route Watch, and lifecycle ownership are unchanged.

## Count ownership

The shared selector owns `consumerVisibleSituationCount`. Raw provider counts and connector-retained counts remain diagnostic/source evidence only.

## Quiet-state ownership

Quiet-state reasoning is centralized in the selector and maps internal states to consumer-safe meanings for: no loaded weather records, provider/connector/source unavailable, stale weather data, all records expired, records filtered outside awareness, and no authoritative fresh local weather. Internal reason codes are audit data only and are not new UI copy.

## County/community ownership

County and community summaries inherit the selected awareness context from the authority snapshot. LP038.3 does not infer locality in UI renderers from broad radius or connector retention.

## Houston parent/child ownership

Houston parent and child regions use the same authority snapshot. LP038.3 adds no Houston-specific weather logic and keeps fallback methods internally visible through audit fields.

## Compatibility owner handling

Legacy compatibility candidates are retained only as fail-open compatibility when the LP038.3 selector is absent. When present, the selector is the first and visible count owner. The LP038.3 audit reports zero remaining visible legacy count owners.

## Current Conditions status

Current Conditions remain unavailable. LP038.3 does not create temperature, wind, precipitation, visibility, station, or locality observations.

## Forecast status

Forecasts remain unavailable. LP038.3 does not create point, grid, zone, county, or local forecast values.

## Consumer language policy

Consumer-visible weather situations use alert title plus location phrasing. Precise ownership uses language such as “Affecting Dayton”; broader or fallback ownership uses language such as “Reported for the Dayton area.” Technical terms such as authority eligibility, source adapter, normalized record, provider record count, polygon owner, county fallback, and raw quiet-state codes are excluded from consumer copy.

## Fallback presentation policy

Fallback ownership remains observable in audits through `fallbackMethodsObserved` and `fallbackDisclosureRequired`, while consumer text avoids presenting broad ownership as precise polygon ownership.

## Freshness ownership

Freshness remains owned by LP038.1/LP038.2 authority. Expired, stale, future-effective, and missing-timestamp records are excluded from consumer-visible active situations.

## Deduplication ownership

Deduplication remains owned by the authority path. Duplicate source IDs are removed, while distinct alerts with identical titles remain separate when their source IDs differ.

## Known limitations

Current Conditions and Forecasts are intentionally not integrated. Legacy bridge candidates still exist for compatibility only when the authority selector is unavailable or has no visible situation.

## Browser audit

Run `window.gridlyLp0383ConsumerWeatherAuthorityMigrationAudit?.()` in the browser. Expected completion status is `CONSUMER_MIGRATION_COMPLETE`, with `recommendedNextMilestone` set to `LP039` and Current Conditions/Forecast integration flags remaining false unless a real source exists.

## Test coverage

`tests/lp0383-consumer-weather-authority-migration.test.js` covers active polygon, county, disclosed fallback, duplicate source IDs, expired, stale, future-effective, missing timestamp, outside-awareness records, distinct same-title alerts, Houston parent/child contexts, Dayton, Livingston, Pasadena, zero records, provider unavailable implications, filtered-outside quiet state, Current Conditions unavailable, and Forecast unavailable.

## Remaining weather roadmap

LP039 can build on the shared consumer selector for any future active conditions, forecast provider integration, or additional weather presentation, but only after authoritative sources are added and certified.
