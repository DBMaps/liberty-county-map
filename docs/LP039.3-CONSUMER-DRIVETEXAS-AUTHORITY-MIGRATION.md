# LP039.3 — Consumer DriveTexas Authority Migration

## Prior consumer owners
The audit found consumer-visible DriveTexas ownership was split across map markers/popups, alert consumers, Awareness Brief enrichment, Community Pulse enrichment, Travel Brief connector reads, Know Before You Go story assembly, active-condition summaries, county/community summaries, Houston summaries, official situation builders, compatibility arrays, retained-record readers, provider readers, connector awareness readers, and rendered marker/row/line counts. These owners could diverge because source records, selected-awareness filters, geography, freshness, deduplication, identity, count ownership, and quiet-state copy were not all delegated to one final consumer contract.

## Migrated consumer owners
LP039.3 establishes `gridlySelectConsumerVisibleDriveTexasSituations()` as the single consumer owner for visible DriveTexas situations and visible DriveTexas counts. Migrated consumers now consume authority-selected situations rather than raw provider records, normalized records, retained connector records, connector awareness records, DOM rows, rendered marker counts, Travel Brief lines, or presentation-only official situation counts.

## Shared consumer selector
The selector derives from `gridlyGetDriveTexasAuthoritySnapshot()` and the LP039.2 proof-backed authority result. It exposes selected awareness context, authority status, unique consumer-visible situations, quiet-state reason and consumer meaning, source availability, fallback flags, freshness status, ownership/roadway/identity methods, category counts, visible category counts, official advisory availability, authority certification, and unproven eligible count. Each visible situation includes a consumer ID, provider ID, category, headline, description, route/roadway, location phrase, locality precision, ownership confidence, freshness timestamp, certified source coordinates, roadway evidence, and location evidence.

## Marker migration
DriveTexas official markers now prefer the shared consumer selector. Marker coordinates come from certified source coordinates on the consumer situation. Existing category styling, marker layer ownership, and click/focus behavior are preserved. Non-DriveTexas community, crossing, weather, and hazard markers are not changed.

## Popup migration
DriveTexas marker popups now use consumer situation content when a consumer situation backs the marker. Popup copy communicates what happened, where it applies, freshness, and DriveTexas official-source status without exposing provider IDs, authority codes, identity methods, raw metadata, normalized fields, proof details, or distance calculations.

## Alert migration
Alert-panel DriveTexas ownership is documented as migrated to the shared selector. Non-DriveTexas community report, weather, crossing, and hazard alert ownership remains unchanged. DriveTexas counts are not owned by DOM row counts.

## Awareness Brief migration
Awareness Brief official roadway evidence delegates through the shared transportation compatibility reader, which now returns consumer-visible authority situations when the LP039.3 selector is present. Raw provider, connector-retained, connector-awareness, and official-situation counts remain diagnostic only.

## Community Pulse migration
Community Pulse DriveTexas/official roadway evidence follows the same shared transportation bridge and therefore delegates to the shared selector for locality, count, freshness, eligibility, and selected-area ownership. Community report evidence ownership is preserved.

## Travel Brief migration
Travel Brief DriveTexas input comes from `gridlyStoryTransportationConnectorRecords()`, which delegates to `gridlySelectConsumerVisibleDriveTexasSituations()` when available. Weather lines, community travel conditions, Route Watch, and roadway runtime are unchanged.

## Know Before You Go and active-condition migration
Know Before You Go story assembly and active-condition/headline evidence use the same shared transportation bridge, preventing a separate official-situation model from establishing DriveTexas authority.

## Count ownership
The visible DriveTexas count owner is `gridlySelectConsumerVisibleDriveTexasSituations().consumerVisibleSituationCount`, representing unique authority-eligible consumer-visible situations. Raw provider, normalized, connector-retained, connector-awareness, official-situation, marker, alert-row, and Travel Brief line counts are diagnostic or presentation counts only.

## Quiet-state ownership
Quiet state is centralized in the selector. Internal reasons distinguish unavailable provider/connector, fetch failure, no loaded records, retained fallback, stale/expired/future/missing timestamps, outside-awareness, unsupported ownership, and no active selected-area situations. Consumer-safe meanings avoid internal codes.

## County/community and Houston ownership
County summaries, community summaries, Houston parent mode, Houston child regions, Spring Branch, and Pasadena all use the selected awareness context in the authority snapshot. LP039.3 does not introduce Harris-wide, corridor-wide, Spring Branch-specific, Pasadena-specific, Houston-specific, or county-name-only eligibility logic.

## Official situation boundary
Official situations remain presentation/enrichment models. They may consume authority-selected situations, but they do not admit source records independently and do not own visible DriveTexas authority or visible counts.

## Compatibility owner handling
Visible compatibility readers either delegate to the shared selector or remain diagnostic. The retained-record, provider-record, and connector-awareness paths cannot repopulate visible DriveTexas situations when the selector returns zero.

## Freshness, identity, deduplication, and eligibility
Freshness, identity, deduplication, coordinate validity, coordinate order, selected-awareness radius proof, category support, and final eligibility remain owned by the LP039.2/LP039.2R1 authority layer. Expired, stale, future-effective, missing-timestamp, invalid-coordinate, reversed-coordinate, outside-awareness, route-name-only, text-only, retained-only, duplicate, missing-identity, and unsupported-category records are excluded from consumer visibility.

## Consumer language and fallback policy
Consumer UI copy describes what happened, where, how recent it is, and that DriveTexas is the official roadway source. It does not expose milestone names, authority eligibility, proof contracts, normalized/provider/retained record labels, identity methods, freshness status codes, fallback codes, exact-source-geometry labels, radius ownership, or raw metadata. LP039.3 does not silently promote fallback-only records.

## Browser audit
`window.gridlyLp0393ConsumerDriveTexasAuthorityMigrationAudit?.()` is passive. It does not fetch, poll, write, use storage, render, move the map, change awareness selection, or mutate source records. It reports the migrated surface flags, diagnostic-count boundaries, selected awareness context, proof/freshness/identity counts, quiet-state meaning, fallback disclosure state, and completion status.

## Deterministic tests
`tests/lp039-3-consumer-drivetexas-authority-migration.test.js` covers active eligible crash, closure, flooding, construction, lane closure, bridge restriction, travel advisory, duplicate provider IDs, duplicate event IDs, distinct same-title records, expired/stale/future/missing timestamp records, invalid/reversed coordinates, outside-awareness records, route-only/text-only records, retained-only evidence, quiet states, marker/alert/Travel Brief authority count ownership, and Houston/Pasadena/Spring Branch surface flags.

## Known limitations
This migration preserves the existing DriveTexas visual design and update boundaries. It does not add new polling, fetches, provider listeners, timers, storage writes, dashboards, technical proof cards, map exploration modes, or non-DriveTexas behavior changes.

## Remaining divergence
None for visible DriveTexas consumer ownership. Diagnostic raw, normalized, retained, connector-awareness, and official-situation counts remain intentionally separate from visible counts.

## Next roadmap recommendation
