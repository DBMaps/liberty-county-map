# Gridly Screenshot Capture Workflow

## Objective

Define a repeatable, planning-only screenshot capture workflow for future store asset production. V277.2 does not create screenshots, device frames, artwork, or binary files.

All captures should preserve Gridly's positioning:

- Awareness Platform First.
- Route Intelligence Second.
- Mobile Portrait Primary.
- Planning and awareness before travel, not turn-by-turn navigation while driving.

## Global Capture Rules

- Capture mobile portrait states only unless a store requirement explicitly needs tablet assets.
- Use current product UI; do not redesign screens for screenshots.
- Use realistic but non-sensitive sample data.
- Avoid personal data, exact private addresses, phone numbers, license plates, faces, and private notes.
- Avoid language that implies official verification, emergency dispatch, live safety guarantees, or government operation.
- Confirm map attribution, status bars, safe areas, and bottom UI are clean.
- Keep headline and caption typography consistent across the final exported screenshot set.
- Re-capture final assets after app icon, splash, and metadata decisions are approved if store framing changes.

## Capture Workflow Steps

1. Confirm the current build is stable and represents the intended submission UI.
2. Prepare representative sample states for each scenario without changing product behavior.
3. Capture the eight core screenshots in the recommended order.
4. Review each capture for safety, privacy, readability, and platform-compliance language.
5. Adapt dimensions and framing separately for Apple and Google in the future asset milestone.
6. Store final generated screenshots only in the planned asset folders during V277.3 or later.

## Screenshot 1 — Awareness Brief

- **Purpose:** Lead with Gridly's core value: quickly understanding local conditions before leaving.
- **Capture state:** Mobile portrait home/awareness state with the awareness brief visible, calm map context, and no modal blocking the primary content.
- **Suggested headline:** Know Before You Go
- **Suggested caption:** See nearby awareness signals before you head out.
- **Acceptance criteria:**
  - Awareness summary is immediately visible.
  - UI reads as an awareness platform, not turn-by-turn navigation.
  - No sensitive location or personal information appears.
  - Text is legible at store-preview sizes.

## Screenshot 2 — Train Blocking Crossing

- **Purpose:** Demonstrate high-value local crossing awareness.
- **Capture state:** Mobile portrait map or alert state showing a train-blocked-crossing report or crossing delay context.
- **Suggested headline:** Spot Crossing Delays
- **Suggested caption:** Review reported train-blocked crossings and local disruptions.
- **Acceptance criteria:**
  - Crossing-related report is clear and prominent.
  - Caption uses report-aware language and does not claim official certainty.
  - Map and marker context are readable.
  - No private address or sensitive location detail is exposed.

## Screenshot 3 — Road Hazard Alert

- **Purpose:** Show road-hazard awareness as practical pre-trip context.
- **Capture state:** Mobile portrait state with a road hazard, closure, flooding, debris, or similar report visible in context.
- **Suggested headline:** Review Road Hazards
- **Suggested caption:** Check community-reported hazards before you travel.
- **Acceptance criteria:**
  - Hazard type is visually understandable.
  - Report language remains community-powered and non-official.
  - UI does not imply emergency routing or guaranteed safety.
  - Important controls and map attribution remain clean.

## Screenshot 4 — Community Reporting

- **Purpose:** Explain how community participation improves awareness.
- **Capture state:** Safe static reporting workflow state with no submitted personal information and no sensitive note content.
- **Suggested headline:** Share Useful Reports
- **Suggested caption:** Add local context when it is safe and lawful.
- **Acceptance criteria:**
  - Reporting action is understandable without showing sensitive data.
  - Safety posture is clear: report only when safe.
  - No keyboard, browser debug UI, or incomplete form errors are visible unless intentionally part of the capture.
  - The screenshot does not encourage interaction while driving.

## Screenshot 5 — Route Watch

- **Purpose:** Present Route Watch as supporting route intelligence, not navigation replacement.
- **Capture state:** Mobile portrait Route Watch state with watched route context or route-awareness panel visible.
- **Suggested headline:** Watch Important Routes
- **Suggested caption:** Follow awareness signals for routes you care about.
- **Acceptance criteria:**
  - Route Watch label or function is clear.
  - No alternate-route, turn-by-turn, or travel-time guarantee is implied.
  - Awareness signals remain the focus.
  - UI is clean in portrait layout.

## Screenshot 6 — Community Pulse

- **Purpose:** Show local activity and community context beyond a single report.
- **Capture state:** Mobile portrait Community Pulse state with recent local activity, signal summary, or pulse-style context visible.
- **Suggested headline:** Follow the Local Pulse
- **Suggested caption:** Understand recent community activity nearby.
- **Acceptance criteria:**
  - Community context is visually distinct from individual incident details.
  - Language avoids official emergency or verified-alert claims.
  - No personally identifying user content is visible.
  - The state supports Awareness Platform First positioning.

## Screenshot 7 — Destination Search

- **Purpose:** Demonstrate planning around a destination before travel.
- **Capture state:** Mobile portrait destination search state with search field, selected result, or nearby awareness context visible.
- **Suggested headline:** Check Your Destination
- **Suggested caption:** Look for awareness signals near where you are headed.
- **Acceptance criteria:**
  - Destination search is presented as planning support.
  - No exact private residence or sensitive destination is shown.
  - The screenshot does not imply turn-by-turn directions.
  - Search result and nearby context are legible.

## Screenshot 8 — Settings

- **Purpose:** Show polish, control, and readiness for real users.
- **Capture state:** Mobile portrait settings state with user-facing options visible and no debug-only controls emphasized.
- **Suggested headline:** Keep Gridly Focused
- **Suggested caption:** Manage preferences for your local awareness experience.
- **Acceptance criteria:**
  - Settings screen looks intentional and stable.
  - Preferences are understandable to non-technical users.
  - No internal-only, debug, or incomplete settings dominate the capture.
  - The screen reinforces trust and user control.
