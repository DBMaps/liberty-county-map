# Gridly Screenshot Plan

## Objective

Plan the screenshots required for App Store and Google Play asset readiness without changing product behavior. Screenshots should demonstrate Gridly's current mobile portrait experience and preserve the product philosophy: Awareness Platform First, Route Intelligence Second.

## Required Screenshots

At minimum, prepare screenshots that show:

1. Awareness Brief.
2. Train Blocking Crossing.
3. Road Hazard Alert.
4. Community Reporting.
5. Route Watch.
6. Community Pulse.
7. Destination Search.
8. Settings.

## Recommended Screenshots

Recommended final set:

### 1. Awareness Brief

Show the primary awareness summary in a clean mobile portrait state. Use this as the opening screenshot because it explains Gridly's core value quickly.

Suggested caption: **Know what is happening nearby before you go.**

### 2. Train Blocking Crossing

Show a clear crossing-related awareness state with a train-blocked-crossing report visible. This should highlight local usefulness without overclaiming certainty.

Suggested caption: **Spot reported crossing delays and local disruptions.**

### 3. Road Hazard Alert

Show a road hazard or closure-style report in context. Keep the map and alert readable.

Suggested caption: **Review community-reported hazards before you travel.**

### 4. Community Reporting

Show the reporting workflow in a safe, static state. Avoid showing personal information or any note that could appear sensitive.

Suggested caption: **Share useful reports when it is safe.**

### 5. Route Watch

Show Route Watch as route intelligence that supports awareness. Do not frame it as alternate routing or turn-by-turn guidance.

Suggested caption: **Watch important routes for awareness signals.**

### 6. Community Pulse

Show recent local activity or community signal context.

Suggested caption: **Follow the pulse of nearby reports.**

### 7. Destination Search

Show destination search as a planning and awareness tool before travel.

Suggested caption: **Check conditions near where you are headed.**

### 8. Settings

Show settings to demonstrate user control and a polished app foundation.

Suggested caption: **Keep Gridly focused on your local awareness needs.**

## Suggested Order

Use this store listing order:

1. Awareness Brief.
2. Train Blocking Crossing.
3. Road Hazard Alert.
4. Route Watch.
5. Community Reporting.
6. Community Pulse.
7. Destination Search.
8. Settings.

This order leads with the product value, then shows practical report types, then demonstrates participation and supporting workflows.

## Capture Guidance

- Capture mobile portrait only.
- Use current production UI; do not redesign screens for V277.1.
- Use realistic but non-sensitive sample data.
- Avoid showing personal data, private addresses, phone numbers, license plates, or exact private locations.
- Do not imply turn-by-turn navigation, alternate routes, background tracking, official emergency support, or government verification.
- Keep captions short, readable, and consistent.
- Capture on representative iPhone and Android viewport sizes for store-specific requirements.
- Verify status bars, safe areas, map attribution, and bottom UI are clean before final export.
- Use the same visual style across all screenshots.
- Re-capture after final icon and splash assets are approved if store framing changes.

## Asset Production Notes

V277.1 is documentation and planning only. Final screenshot capture, device framing, platform-specific dimensions, and image exports should occur in V277.2 — Store Asset Production.
