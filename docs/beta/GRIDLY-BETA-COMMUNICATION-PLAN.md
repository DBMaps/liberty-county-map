# GRIDLY BETA COMMUNICATION PLAN

## Status

V900

## Purpose

This plan keeps beta communication simple, human, and consistent as Gridly expands in controlled cohorts.

## Communication Principles

- Keep messages simple and human.
- Avoid technical language.
- Avoid overpromising.
- Explain that Gridly helps with awareness and does not replace official emergency sources.
- Ask for honest feedback about clarity, trust, and usefulness.

## Welcome Message Cadence

Send a short welcome when a tester is invited. Include the beta URL, what to try, where to send feedback, and the safety reminder.

## Week 1 Check-In

Ask what was clear, what was confusing, whether install/open worked, and whether Gridly helped them know what to check before going.

## Week 2 Check-In

Ask whether the tester used Gridly again, what would make it easier to trust, and whether any wording or map behavior felt confusing.

## Release Update Cadence

Send updates only when helpful. Focus on what changed for testers, not technical implementation.

## Urgent Issue Communication

If a trust, location, reporting, or install issue is discovered, tell affected testers plainly:

- What to watch for.
- Whether to pause use.
- When an update is available.
- That official sources should always be followed.

## Known Limitation Reminders

Repeat these reminders when needed:

- Gridly is in beta.
- Community reports may be incomplete.
- Conditions can change quickly.
- Gridly is not for emergencies.
- Official sources and local authorities should be followed.

## Feedback Acknowledgement

Acknowledge feedback quickly, especially for trust, reporting, installation, location, and safety concerns.

## Tester Re-Engagement

If testers go quiet, send a simple reminder asking them to open Gridly before a normal trip and share one thing that felt useful or confusing.

## Beta Closure / Next-Phase Message

When a cohort closes or the next phase begins, thank testers, summarize what was learned in plain language, and explain whether the beta is pausing, continuing, or expanding.

## Message Templates

### Welcome

Hi! We are inviting a small group to test Gridly. Gridly is built around **Know Before You Go** and helps with local awareness before you head out. Please try this beta link and tell us what feels clear, useful, or confusing: https://dbmaps.github.io/liberty-county-map/?v=beta-clean-4

Gridly is in beta and does not replace official emergency sources. For emergencies, call 911 or follow local officials.

### Week 1 Check-In

Thanks again for testing Gridly. Did it open okay on your phone? Was anything confusing? Did it help you understand what to check before leaving?

### Week 2 Check-In

Have you opened Gridly again since the first test? If yes, what made you trust it or not trust it? If no, what would make it more useful to check?

### Feedback Acknowledgement

Thank you. I captured that feedback. If you can, please send your phone type, browser or install mode, and a screenshot if it is safe and easy.

### Urgent Limitation Reminder

Quick note: Gridly is still in beta. If something looks wrong or unclear, please do not rely on it for emergency decisions. Always follow official sources and local authorities.

### Next Phase

Thank you for helping with this beta round. Your feedback is helping us decide what to fix, what to explain better, and whether Gridly is ready for the next small group of testers.
