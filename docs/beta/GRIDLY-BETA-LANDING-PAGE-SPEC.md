# Gridly Beta Landing Page Spec

## Purpose

The beta landing page gives testers everything they need before opening Gridly. It should make the beta feel simple, trustworthy, premium, and consumer-friendly while setting clear expectations about what Gridly can and cannot do.

## Mission

**Know Before You Go.** Gridly helps people understand nearby road, weather, and community awareness conditions before they leave.

## Product Philosophy

1. **Awareness Platform First** — help testers understand what is happening around them.
2. **Route Intelligence Second** — route-related features support awareness, but Gridly does not replace judgment, official alerts, or local road authority guidance.

## Install Button Location

The landing page should place the primary install action above the fold:

- Label: **Open Gridly Beta**
- Secondary label, where supported: **Install Gridly**
- Placement: centered in the primary hero area, followed by concise install guidance.
- Repeat location: once again near the bottom after FAQ and support guidance.

## Platform Support

Gridly beta is designed mobile-first with **mobile portrait as the primary experience**.

Supported tester targets:

- Modern iPhone browsers
- Modern Android browsers
- Desktop browsers for review and support only

Desktop can be used to preview the beta, but real-world validation should prioritize a phone in portrait orientation.

## Requirements

Testers should have:

- A modern phone browser
- A stable internet connection
- Location permission available when they want local awareness
- Willingness to submit feedback when something is confusing, incorrect, or broken
- Understanding that Gridly is beta software and may include incomplete or changing features

## Known Limitations

The landing page should summarize known limitations without overwhelming testers:

- Community reports may be incomplete, delayed, duplicated, or inaccurate.
- Location permission is optional but improves local awareness.
- External data sources can be delayed or unavailable.
- Gridly is not an emergency notification service.
- Gridly does not replace official transportation, law enforcement, weather, or emergency-management sources.
- Some install prompts vary by browser and device.

## How to Send Feedback

The landing page should provide a clear feedback path:

1. Use the beta feedback channel provided in the tester invitation.
2. Include device type, browser, approximate time, and what the tester expected to happen.
3. Include screenshots when possible.
4. Flag safety-critical confusion immediately.

## Privacy Statement

Gridly should explain privacy in plain language:

- Testers should only submit information they are comfortable sharing.
- Location access is used to improve local awareness when the tester allows it.
- Community reports should not include private personal information.
- Feedback may be reviewed by the Gridly team to improve the beta experience.

## Community Reporting Disclaimer

Community reports are tester-submitted awareness signals. They may be wrong, incomplete, stale, or unverified. Testers should use community reports as situational context, not as the only basis for travel or safety decisions.

## Official-Source Disclaimer

Gridly does not replace official sources. For emergencies, road closures, evacuation instructions, severe weather warnings, or public-safety decisions, testers should follow official emergency services, transportation agencies, weather authorities, and local government guidance.

## Expected Experience

A successful landing page should help testers understand:

- What Gridly is
- Why the beta exists
- How to open or install it
- What permissions may appear
- What to try first
- Where to get help
- What Gridly does not promise

The tone should be calm, direct, and polished. Avoid internal engineering language, lengthy technical explanations, or unsupported claims.

## FAQ

### What is Gridly?

Gridly is a community awareness experience that helps testers understand nearby conditions before leaving.

### Is Gridly a navigation app?

Not primarily. Gridly is awareness first and route intelligence second.

### Do I need to install it?

No. Testers can open the beta link in a browser. Installing, when available, can make repeat access easier.

### Do I have to allow location?

No. Location permission improves nearby awareness, but testers can still review the beta without granting it.

### Are community reports verified?

Community reports are not guaranteed to be verified. They are awareness signals and should be treated as context.

### What should I test first?

Open the beta, complete onboarding, choose an awareness area, review the Awareness Brief, and submit one appropriate test report if conditions allow.

### What if something looks wrong?

Send feedback with the device, browser, time, steps taken, and screenshots if possible.

## Contact Guidance

The page should direct testers to the official beta feedback channel from their invite. If the issue involves immediate safety, testers should contact the relevant official authority first, then share Gridly feedback when safe.

## Success Criteria

The landing page is successful when:

- Testers understand the mission before opening Gridly.
- The beta URL is easy to find and open.
- Install expectations are clear for iPhone, Android, and browser-only use.
- Testers understand privacy, community-report, and official-source limits.
- Testers know how to send useful feedback.
- The page feels polished, simple, trustworthy, and consumer-friendly.
