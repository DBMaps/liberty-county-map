# GRIDLY BETA SUCCESS METRICS

## Status

V900

## Purpose

These metrics help Gridly decide whether controlled beta expansion is healthy, whether to pause, and what to improve next.

## Why Metrics Matter

Do not judge success only by number of users. Trust, comprehension, and reliability matter more than raw growth. User confusion is a valid product signal.

## Core Beta Metrics

- Install success rate
- Onboarding completion
- First successful open
- Report submission success
- Awareness comprehension
- Perceived trust
- Weekly active testers
- Feedback response time
- Unresolved P0/P1 issues
- Tester retention

## Suggested Starting Targets

- Install success rate: 80% or better for testers who attempt install.
- Onboarding completion: 85% or better among new testers.
- First successful open: 95% or better from invited testers who try the link.
- Report submission success: no unresolved P0/P1 reporting failures.
- Awareness comprehension: most testers can explain Gridly as awareness before route intelligence.
- Perceived trust: testers understand limits and do not mistake Gridly for official emergency information.
- Weekly active testers: enough recurring testers to show repeat usefulness, not just first-click curiosity.
- Feedback response time: acknowledge important feedback within 1-2 days when possible.
- Unresolved P0/P1 issues: zero open P0; P1 issues tracked with owner and decision.
- Tester retention: early testers continue checking Gridly after initial invite.

## Qualitative Signals

- Testers say Gridly helps them know what to check before leaving.
- Testers understand that quiet maps do not guarantee clear conditions.
- Testers can describe what confused them.
- Testers trust the app more when limitations are clear.
- Local testers recognize familiar areas and understand the map context.

## Red Flags

- Testers believe Gridly replaces official alerts or emergency services.
- Multiple testers cannot install or reopen Gridly.
- Multiple testers misunderstand report locations.
- Feedback is coming in faster than it can be reviewed.
- People outside the intended cohort are receiving the beta link.
- P0/P1 issues remain open while expansion continues.

## Metrics Not To Overvalue

- Total invited users.
- One-time link clicks.
- Feature requests without repeated evidence.
- Praise without actual repeat use.
- Broad geographic reach before local confidence is high.
