# GRIDLY BETA ROLLOUT PLAN

## Status

V900

## Purpose

This plan defines how Gridly should expand from internal family testing into controlled beta cohorts without creating a rushed public launch. It builds on the V899 beta materials and turns them into an operating plan for who to invite, when to expand, when to pause, and how to use feedback.

## Mission

**Know Before You Go**

Gridly helps people check nearby conditions before they leave, while planning a route, or when they need local awareness.

## Product Philosophy

**Awareness Platform First**  
**Route Intelligence Second**

The beta should validate whether people understand Gridly as a simple awareness tool before it is evaluated as a broader route intelligence product.

## Rollout Philosophy

- Do not invite everyone at once.
- Expand in small, controlled cohorts.
- Let feedback drive the next step.
- Keep Liberty / Dayton as the center of gravity until confidence is high.
- Add counties only after stability, support process, and feedback handling are proven.
- Protect the polished product experience already established for beta.
- Treat beta expansion as an operational process, not a marketing blast.

The first confidence area remains Dayton / Liberty County. Southeast Texas expansion may be planned, but it should remain controlled until the support process, issue triage process, and product stability are proven.

## What V900 Is

V900 is the beta rollout-control package. It establishes documents, checklists, gates, and operating rules for controlled tester expansion.

## What V900 Is Not

- It is not a general-public launch.
- It is not an emergency-service readiness certification.
- It is not a claim that Gridly provides official real-time emergency information.
- It is not a runtime feature release.
- It is not a change to reporting, alerts, hazard lifecycle, onboarding, installation, Route Watch, search, weather, community intelligence, crossings, or directional intelligence.

## Rollout Phases

### Phase 0: Internal Family Testing

Goal: confirm the current beta is understandable and stable for people close to the product.

Recommended activity:

- Use the current beta URL on normal phones.
- Confirm install/open behavior.
- Collect simple feedback in one place.
- Watch for trust, location, installation, onboarding, and reporting concerns.

### Phase 1: Friends and Family Controlled Beta

Goal: invite a small number of trusted testers who are patient and communicative.

Recommended activity:

- Invite only people who understand this is a beta.
- Ask for clear feedback after first use and after several days.
- Keep the group small enough that every issue can be reviewed.

### Phase 2: Local Community Beta

Goal: validate Gridly with local Dayton / Liberty County users who understand the area.

Recommended activity:

- Invite local users who can recognize whether locations and local wording make sense.
- Prioritize people likely to give useful feedback instead of only casual opinions.
- Keep reminders clear that Gridly is for awareness and does not replace official sources.

### Phase 3: Southeast Texas Controlled Regional Beta

Goal: test broader regional usefulness without losing operational control.

Recommended activity:

- Expand only when local feedback is manageable.
- Track each invited region or county.
- Do not add additional counties unless support handling and stability are proven.

### Phase 4: Broader Public Beta Candidate

Goal: prepare for a future public beta decision after controlled cohorts show confidence.

Recommended activity:

- Confirm there are no unresolved P0/P1 issues.
- Confirm feedback flow is working.
- Confirm support expectations are realistic.
- Confirm user-facing language does not overpromise.

This phase is only a candidate stage. V900 does not certify a broad public launch.

## Expansion Gates

Before moving to the next phase, confirm:

- No unresolved P0 launch blockers.
- No unresolved P1 trust or core workflow issues that affect normal beta use.
- Feedback is captured in a single tracking place.
- Testers understand Gridly is community awareness, not an emergency service.
- Install and first open are understandable for the current cohort.
- Reporting and location behavior remain trustworthy.
- Denise can reasonably support the next cohort size.

## Pause Conditions

Pause new invitations if any of the following occur:

- Incorrect location placement creates user confusion or trust risk.
- Reporting appears broken or unreliable.
- Onboarding or install flow blocks multiple testers.
- Testers believe Gridly is official emergency information.
- A protected system may need investigation.
- Feedback volume exceeds the ability to review and respond.
- A public audience begins sharing the beta beyond the intended cohort.

## Roles and Responsibilities

- **Denise / Beta Operator:** invite testers, track cohorts, collect feedback, decide when to pause or expand.
- **Tester:** use Gridly normally, report clear feedback, avoid relying on Gridly for emergencies.
- **Issue Reviewer:** convert feedback into actionable issues and set priority.
- **Codex Work Owner:** prepare narrow, audit-first work requests when an issue needs implementation.
- **Release Reviewer:** confirm documentation, issue priority, and merge readiness before expansion.

## Beta Exit Criteria

Controlled beta may be considered ready for a broader public beta candidate only when:

- Core beta metrics meet starting targets for more than one review cycle.
- P0/P1 issues are resolved or accepted with clear limitations.
- Testers understand the product purpose.
- Feedback loops are consistent and not chaotic.
- Local confidence is high in Liberty / Dayton.
- Known limitations are clearly communicated.

## Recommended Next Milestones

1. Finish Phase 0 internal family testing notes.
2. Invite a small Phase 1 friends-and-family cohort.
3. Review feedback weekly using the V900 metrics template.
4. Resolve trust, installation, reporting, and location issues before expanding.
5. Prepare a Phase 2 local Dayton / Liberty County cohort only after Phase 1 is stable.
