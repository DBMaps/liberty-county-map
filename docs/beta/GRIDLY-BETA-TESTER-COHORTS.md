# GRIDLY BETA TESTER COHORTS

## Status

V900

## Purpose

This guide defines who should be invited into each Gridly beta cohort and who should wait until the product and support process are ready for a larger audience.

## Tester Selection Principles

Early testers should be:

- Patient with beta software.
- Communicative when something is confusing.
- Local enough to understand Dayton / Liberty County context.
- Comfortable sharing device, browser, and installation details.
- Clear that Gridly is community awareness, not a replacement for official emergency sources.

## Cohort A: Internal / Family

Suggested size: 3-8 testers.

Purpose: confirm basic clarity, install flow, first open, and trust signals with people closest to the project.

## Cohort B: Trusted Friends

Suggested size: 5-15 testers.

Purpose: add trusted outside eyes while keeping feedback easy to manage.

## Cohort C: Local Dayton / Liberty County Testers

Suggested size: 15-40 testers.

Purpose: validate local comprehension, map context, location confidence, and everyday usefulness in the center-of-gravity area.

## Cohort D: Community Operator Testers

Suggested size: 5-20 testers.

Purpose: gather feedback from people who frequently coordinate local activity, commute planning, school pickup, neighborhood updates, business operations, or community awareness.

## Cohort E: Southeast Texas Regional Testers

Suggested size: 25-75 testers, added in small batches.

Purpose: test regional relevance after local support and issue handling are stable.

## Cohort F: Public Beta Candidate

Suggested size: limited public access only after prior cohorts pass expansion gates.

Purpose: prepare for a broader beta decision. This is not a public launch certification.

## Who Should Be Invited First

- Internal family testers already helping with beta.
- Trusted friends who will give specific feedback.
- Local Dayton / Liberty County users who know the area.
- People who can test on both browser and installed app modes.
- People who will report confusion without expecting everything to be final.

## Who Should Not Be Invited Yet

- Large public audiences.
- High-risk users who expect emergency-service reliability.
- People likely to treat Gridly as official emergency information.
- Users who need guaranteed real-time official data.
- Audiences that may broadly share the beta link before support is ready.

## Tester Expectations

Testers should understand:

- Gridly is in controlled beta.
- Gridly helps with community awareness.
- Gridly does not replace official sources, 911, emergency alerts, law enforcement, fire, EMS, school, county, city, or road authority instructions.
- Reports may be incomplete or change quickly.
- Feedback is valuable even when it is about confusion or trust.

## Tester Onboarding Checklist

- Share the current beta URL.
- Explain the mission: Know Before You Go.
- Explain that Gridly is awareness first, route intelligence second.
- Remind testers not to use Gridly for emergencies.
- Ask testers to note device type, browser, and install mode.
- Ask testers to send screenshots when helpful and safe.
- Tell testers where to send feedback.

## Expansion Decision Checklist

Before inviting the next cohort:

- Are current testers able to open Gridly successfully?
- Are install and first-run questions manageable?
- Are any location, reporting, onboarding, or trust issues unresolved?
- Are testers giving feedback through the chosen intake path?
- Is Denise able to respond without losing track?
- Is Liberty / Dayton confidence high enough to expand?
- Would a larger cohort create support chaos?
