# Gridly Beta Tester Guide

## What Gridly Is

Gridly is a community awareness platform built around a simple idea: **Know Before You Go**.

Gridly helps people see nearby reports about blocked crossings, road hazards, flooding, closures, and changing travel conditions before they leave or while they are planning a trip. It is an awareness tool first, with route intelligence second.

Gridly is in beta. Reports depend on community activity, and conditions can change quickly. Gridly is not for emergencies. For emergencies, always call 911 or local emergency services.

## What To Test

As a beta tester, please use Gridly like you would on a normal day:

- Check the app before leaving home, work, school, or a local stop.
- Look for reports near places you know.
- Review the map and awareness surfaces.
- Submit a report only when you can do so safely.
- Open Settings and review the help, install, feedback, privacy, and About Gridly areas.

## How To Use First Run

When Gridly opens for the first time, follow the short walkthrough. It explains what Gridly does, how location awareness works, and how to start with a nearby area.

If you want to see the walkthrough again, open **Settings**, go to **About & Support**, and choose **Show walkthrough again**.

## How To Use Awareness

Awareness gives you a quick read on nearby community activity. Use it to answer:

- Is anything nearby worth checking before I go?
- Are there recent reports in the area?
- Does the app help me understand what to do next?

## How To Use Map

Use the map to look around your area. Tap markers or visible reports to learn more. The map is meant to help you understand where activity is being reported, not to replace your judgment or traffic laws.

## How To Use Alerts

Alerts help highlight important reported conditions. Review the wording, severity, and freshness. Tell us if an alert feels clear, confusing, too strong, or not strong enough.

## How To Submit Reports

Submit reports only when it is safe. Choose the best matching report type, add helpful location context if needed, and keep wording simple. Do not use Gridly to report emergencies.

## How To Use Settings

Settings includes preferences, install guidance, feedback, privacy, version information, and About Gridly. Please check that the text is readable, buttons look normal, and nothing appears broken or encoded incorrectly.

## What Feedback Is Useful

Useful feedback includes:

- What confused you.
- What felt slow.
- What felt helpful.
- What wording did not make sense.
- Whether you trusted what Gridly showed.
- Whether you knew what to do next.
- Anything that looked broken.
- Your device, browser, and whether Gridly was installed to your home screen.
