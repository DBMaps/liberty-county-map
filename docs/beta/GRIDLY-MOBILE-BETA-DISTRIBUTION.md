# Gridly Mobile Beta Distribution

## Purpose

This document explains how the Gridly Mobile Portrait beta should be distributed in a controlled, understandable, and supportable way.

## Invitation Strategy

Invite small groups first. Start with trusted testers who understand that Gridly is beta, Mobile Portrait is the only supported platform, and feedback is more important than polished public promotion.

## QR Code Recommendations

- Encode only the current beta URL.
- Pair the QR code with the words **Mobile Portrait beta only**.
- Place QR codes where testers can scan from a phone.
- Replace QR codes when the beta URL changes.
- Avoid placing QR codes in contexts that imply public emergency readiness.

## Email Invitations

Email invitations should include:

- A short mission statement: **Know Before You Go**.
- The current beta URL.
- A note that Gridly supports Mobile Portrait only.
- A reminder that desktop and landscape remain gated.
- The tester checklist.
- The feedback destination.

## Text Invitations

Text invitations should be brief:

> Try the Gridly Mobile Portrait beta: https://dbmaps.github.io/liberty-county-map/?v=beta-clean-4. Gridly helps with local awareness before you go. It is beta, not for emergencies, and desktop/landscape are not supported yet.

## Family Testing

Family testing is useful for early clarity checks. Ask family testers to describe what they thought Gridly was for, where they got confused, and whether they could install and relaunch it without coaching.

## Community Testing

Community testing should remain controlled. Use clear invitations, small cohorts, and direct support follow-up. Do not present Gridly as an official emergency source or a finished public service.

## County Rollout Strategy

Keep the current beta county focus clear. Expand to additional counties only after readiness, support, data, and communications are validated through separate rollout decisions.

## Version Verification

When collecting feedback, ask testers to provide:

- The beta URL they opened.
- Whether Gridly was opened from browser or home screen.
- Device model if known.
- Browser.
- Screenshot of version or About information when available.

## Support Workflow

1. Receive feedback or issue report.
2. Confirm device, browser, orientation, and beta URL.
3. Confirm whether the tester used browser or home-screen launch.
4. Reproduce only within the supported Mobile Portrait beta scope.
5. Classify the issue as install, onboarding, awareness, reporting, settings, wording, or unsupported platform.
6. Escalate only validated issues with enough detail to act.

## Expansion Guidance

Expand only when:

- Testers can complete install and onboarding with minimal support.
- Feedback confirms Mobile Portrait expectations are clear.
- Known limitations are understood.
- Support can handle the next cohort.
- The rollout owner approves the next group.

## Success Criteria

Distribution succeeds when testers receive the right URL, understand Mobile Portrait support, avoid desktop and landscape expectations, complete the checklist, and submit actionable feedback through the support workflow.
