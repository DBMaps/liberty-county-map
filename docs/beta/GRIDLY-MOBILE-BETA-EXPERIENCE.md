# Gridly Mobile Beta Experience

## Purpose

This document describes the complete Gridly mobile beta experience for V902. The milestone strengthens how testers understand, install, launch, and evaluate Gridly without changing application runtime behavior.

## Mission

Gridly exists to help people **Know Before You Go**. The beta should help testers understand local conditions before they leave, while remembering that Gridly is community awareness and not an official emergency source.

## Product Philosophy

Gridly is an awareness platform first and route intelligence second. The beta experience should make local awareness easy to understand before asking users to think about route decisions.

## Supported Platform

Gridly currently supports **Mobile Portrait only**.

## Gated Platforms

Desktop and landscape remain intentionally gated. V902 does not enable desktop, does not enable landscape, and does not suggest either platform is supported during beta.

## Current Beta URL

Current beta URL:

<https://dbmaps.github.io/liberty-county-map/?v=beta-clean-4>

## Who Beta Is For

The mobile beta is for testers who:

- Use Gridly on a phone in portrait orientation.
- Can follow a simple install and onboarding flow.
- Understand that beta feedback is the goal.
- Can report what feels clear, useful, confusing, broken, or incomplete.
- Are comfortable testing a community awareness product that is still improving.

## Who Beta Is Not For

The mobile beta is not for people who:

- Need official emergency information.
- Expect desktop support.
- Expect landscape support.
- Expect full public-launch completeness.
- Need Gridly to replace 911, local officials, road agencies, or emergency alerts.

## Mobile-First Philosophy

Gridly is designed around the way people check conditions in real life: quickly, on a phone, before leaving or while planning a trip. Mobile-first testing keeps the beta focused on the supported experience instead of spreading feedback across platforms that remain gated.

## Portrait-First Philosophy

Portrait orientation is the supported beta posture because it matches normal phone use, simplifies installation and onboarding guidance, and keeps map, awareness, reporting, and settings feedback comparable across testers.

## Current County Focus

The current beta remains focused on Liberty County awareness. Broader county expansion should happen only through controlled rollout decisions and should not be implied by V902.

## Expected User Journey

1. Tester receives the current beta URL.
2. Tester opens Gridly on a phone in portrait orientation.
3. Tester optionally installs Gridly to the home screen.
4. Tester launches Gridly from the browser or home-screen icon.
5. Tester completes onboarding.
6. Tester selects or confirms an awareness area.
7. Tester grants location if comfortable.
8. Tester reviews the map and Awareness Brief.
9. Tester submits one safe test report if appropriate.
10. Tester checks Community Pulse and Settings.
11. Tester sends feedback about clarity, trust, usefulness, and any issues.

## Known Limitations

- Gridly is beta software.
- Mobile Portrait is the only supported beta experience.
- Desktop remains intentionally gated.
- Landscape remains intentionally gated.
- Community reports depend on tester participation.
- Gridly does not replace official emergency sources.
- Conditions can change faster than community reports appear.
- Notifications and device capabilities may vary by phone and browser.

## Success Criteria

V902 succeeds when:

- Testers understand that Gridly currently supports Mobile Portrait only.
- Testers understand that desktop and landscape remain intentionally gated.
- Testers can open, install, launch, onboard, select an awareness area, and provide feedback.
- Beta materials reinforce awareness-first and route-intelligence-second positioning.
- No runtime behavior, protected systems, or application logic is changed.
