# Gridly Mobile First Principles

## Purpose

This document explains why Gridly is mobile-first during beta and how that principle protects product clarity.

## Mission Alignment

Gridly helps people **Know Before You Go**. That mission naturally starts on the phone because people check conditions while preparing to leave, riding as passengers, or deciding whether to delay a trip.

## Portrait-First Design

Portrait-first design keeps the beta focused on the most common phone posture. It supports quick reading, one-handed use, simple onboarding, and consistent tester feedback.

## Consumer Simplicity

A mobile-first beta reduces complexity. Testers receive one supported posture, one install path family, and one clear expectation: use Gridly on a phone in portrait orientation.

## Awareness-First Philosophy

Gridly is an awareness platform first and route intelligence second. The mobile experience should help people understand nearby conditions before asking them to make routing decisions.

## Real-World Usage

Most beta checks happen close to departure time. Mobile Portrait supports quick glances at the map, Awareness Brief, Community Pulse, reports, and Settings without requiring a desk or larger screen.

## Why Desktop Remains Gated

Desktop remains gated because V902 is not a desktop milestone. Enabling or implying desktop support would widen the test surface, create unsupported expectations, and distract from validating the supported mobile beta experience.

## Why Landscape Remains Gated

Landscape remains gated because V902 is not a landscape milestone. Keeping landscape unavailable protects layout consistency, tester comparability, and product focus during beta.

## Future Expansion Philosophy

Future platform expansion should be intentional, certified, and supported by dedicated design, validation, documentation, and support readiness. Expansion should not happen by implication through beta materials.

## Guiding Principles

- Support Mobile Portrait clearly.
- Keep desktop gated until explicitly certified.
- Keep landscape gated until explicitly certified.
- Explain beta limitations plainly.
- Design for real-world pre-trip awareness.
- Preserve awareness-first and route-intelligence-second positioning.
- Avoid runtime changes unless a future milestone explicitly authorizes them.
