# V445 — Historical Awareness Language Expansion Review

## 1. Executive Summary

**Answer: Limited Yes.**

Gridly can safely expand historical awareness language beyond the current V434–V439 phrase set only in a narrow, adapter-governed catalog that stays secondary, past-observation focused, and non-predictive. The expansion is safe only when Historical Intelligence is translated into **Historical Awareness** language rather than exposed as raw history, pattern analytics, routing evidence, reliability scoring, or forecast language.

This review does not authorize implementation. It creates a governance catalog for future adapter-controlled work.

### Decision summary

- **Historical Presence:** approved.
- **Soft Recurrence Awareness:** approved only when phrased as observed prior similar reports, not future likelihood.
- **Historical Resolution Awareness:** conditional at most; direct clearance or duration language remains too risky.
- **Activity Pattern Awareness:** conditional only when all time-window specificity is removed.
- **Historical Context Support:** approved for limited-evidence and awareness-support framing.

The safe path remains: **Awareness Platform First, Route Intelligence Second.** Historical Intelligence may support awareness; it must not become prediction, forecasting, route guidance, navigation, reliability scoring, reputation scoring, raw history browsing, a dashboard, or an API.

## 2. Current Approved Language Inventory

Current approved adapter language remains intentionally small and conservative.

| Phrase | Current status | Why approved |
|---|---|---|
| “Community reports have occurred here before.” | Approved | Uses past-tense observation language; avoids counts, timestamps, future claims, severity, reliability, routing advice, and source metadata. |
| “Repeated reports have been observed here.” | Approved | Communicates prior repeated community observation without exact volume, timing, prediction, or route instruction. |
| “Previous community reports have been observed nearby.” | Approved | Allows nearby historical context while avoiding raw location history and keeping the claim low-specificity. |
| “Historical evidence is still limited.” | Approved caveat | Prevents overclaiming in low-evidence situations and supports suppression-first behavior. |

These phrases are approved because they describe **past community observations** only. They do not say what will happen, how long a condition will last, which route to choose, whether a location is reliable or unreliable, how many reports exist, or when the last report occurred.

## 3. Candidate Historical Language Review

### Category A — Historical Presence

| Candidate phrase | Classification | Reasoning |
|---|---|---|
| “Community reports have occurred here before.” | **APPROVED** | Already approved. Past-tense, low-claim, awareness-supporting, and non-predictive. |
| “Repeated reports have been observed here.” | **APPROVED** | Already approved. Indicates prior observations without raw counts or a future claim. |
| “Previous community reports have been observed nearby.” | **APPROVED** | Safe when used as secondary context and not converted into a location reputation claim. |
| “Drivers previously reported this road issue.” | **CONDITIONAL** | Past-tense, but “drivers” can imply verified traffic authority or broad consensus. Prefer “community reports” unless surface context clearly supports driver language. |

**Review status:** Historical Presence is safe for adapter-controlled awareness expansion when phrased as non-specific past community observation.

### Category B — Recurrence Awareness

| Candidate phrase | Classification | Reasoning |
|---|---|---|
| “Recurring reports have been observed here.” | **CONDITIONAL** | Can imply future recurrence. May be acceptable only if adapter thresholds are strong and visual hierarchy keeps it secondary. |
| “This location has experienced similar reports before.” | **APPROVED** | Safer than “recurring” because it emphasizes similarity in past reports rather than a pattern that may continue. |
| “Similar community reports have been observed here before.” | **APPROVED** | Past-observation focused and avoids “recurring,” “common,” or “frequent.” |
| “Recurring road issue reported here.” | **NOT APPROVED** | Too label-like and can create a location reputation or ongoing-condition impression. |
| “Drivers have repeatedly reported activity here.” | **CONDITIONAL** | Repeated history may be acceptable, but “drivers” and “activity” are broad; requires adapter context and safer wording. |

**Review status:** Recurrence can be translated only into softened awareness language. Strong recurrence labels are not approved.

### Category C — Historical Resolution Awareness

| Candidate phrase | Classification | Reasoning |
|---|---|---|
| “Previous reports at this location have cleared after observed intervals.” | **CONDITIONAL** | Past-tense and non-specific, but users may infer future clearance timing. Requires strict secondary placement and no durations. |
| “Similar reports have eventually cleared.” | **CONDITIONAL** | Lower specificity than duration claims, but “eventually” can still imply reassurance or waiting advice. |
| “Previous community reports here included resolution updates.” | **CONDITIONAL** | Safer than clearance timing, but still requires review because it introduces resolution history as a consumer concept. |
| “Usually clears in 1 hour.” | **NOT APPROVED** | Direct prediction/ETA implication and route-decision risk. |
| “Typical delay 25 minutes.” | **NOT APPROVED** | Converts history into route-delay expectation. |

**Review status:** Historical Resolution Awareness should not be part of the initial expanded approved catalog. It may be studied later only as non-specific, non-actionable context.

### Category D — Activity Pattern Awareness

| Candidate phrase | Classification | Reasoning |
|---|---|---|

| “Reports have often occurred during similar periods.” | **NOT APPROVED** | “Often” plus period-matching implies a time-based forecast or planning cue. |
| “Activity has historically clustered in this time window.” | **NOT APPROVED** | Time-window clustering creates prediction and route-planning risk. |
| “Previous activity has been observed in this area.” | **APPROVED** | Generic, past-tense, and does not expose time windows or counts. |
| “Community reports have appeared in this area before.” | **APPROVED** | Past-observation focused and low-claim. |

**Review status:** Generic activity awareness is safe. Time-pattern language is not approved.

### Category E — Historical Context Support

| Candidate phrase | Classification | Reasoning |
|---|---|---|
| “Historical evidence supports ongoing community awareness.” | **CONDITIONAL** | Awareness-oriented, but “supports” can sound evidentiary or confidence-like. Use only if paired with current awareness and no scoring. |
| “Historical evidence remains limited.” | **APPROVED** | Safe low-evidence caveat and compatible with suppression-first behavior. |
| “Historical evidence is still limited.” | **APPROVED** | Existing approved caveat. |
| “Past community reports add context here.” | **APPROVED** | Clearly contextual and avoids prediction, counts, timing, and routing advice. |
| “Historical context is available for this location.” | **NOT APPROVED** | Suggests a history browser, drill-down, or dashboard. |

**Review status:** Context-support language is safe when it remains a caveat or short secondary awareness line. It must not imply browsable history.

## 4. Explicitly Prohibited Language

The following language must never be adapter-approved for consumer-visible historical awareness.

| Prohibited language | Why prohibited |
|---|---|
| “Usually clears in 1 hour.” | Converts history into an implied clearance forecast or waiting recommendation. |
| “Most reported between 6 PM and 8 PM.” | Exposes time-window patterning and can be read as planning or prediction guidance. |
| “Reported 12 times.” | Exposes raw counts and creates significance, reputation, or dashboard behavior. |
| “Last reported 3 days ago.” | Exposes raw recency and invites users to infer current likelihood or reliability. |
| “Expect delays.” | Direct route-impact prediction and route guidance. |
| “Likely to clear.” | Future-likelihood claim and implied forecast. |
| “Forecasted risk.” | Explicit forecasting, outside historical awareness boundaries. |
| “Confidence: 91%.” | Numeric scoring creates false precision and decision authority. |
| “Reliability score.” | Scoring/reputation system, not awareness support. |
| “Community reliability.” | Reporter/community reputation scoring and trust ranking. |
| “Location reliability.” | Location reputation scoring and route-decision authority. |
| “Frequent issue area.” | Creates a hotspot/reputation label and future-risk inference. |
| “Avoid this area.” | Navigation and route guidance. |
| “Best alternate route.” | Navigation and routing recommendation. |
| “Historical dashboard available.” | Raw history browsing / dashboard implication. |

## 7. Consumer Understanding Assessment

| Phrase type | Likely consumer interpretation | Finding |
|---|---|---|
| Simple historical presence | Awareness | Generally understood as context. Low risk when secondary. |
| Soft similar-report language | Awareness with mild recurrence inference | Acceptable if not paired with severity, route, duration, or time-window claims. |
| “Recurring” language | Awareness / prediction blend | Requires review because many consumers may infer the condition is likely to happen again. |
| Resolution language | Prediction / reassurance | Risky because consumers may treat past clearance as a clue about current clearance. |
| Time-pattern language | Prediction / routing advice | Not safe because users can plan around implied historical windows. |
| Count or recency language | Certainty / raw history browsing | Not safe because it creates false precision and dashboard expectations. |
| Reliability or confidence language | Certainty / scoring | Not safe because it creates trust-ranking or reputation systems. |
| Route-impact language | Routing advice | Not safe because it exceeds awareness support. |
| Low-evidence caveats | Awareness / uncertainty | Safe because they reduce overconfidence. |

## 9. Adapter Expansion Feasibility

**Answer: Limited Yes.**

The Historical Awareness Adapter can safely support a larger phrase catalog if expansion is limited to the approved catalog above and remains governed by adapter-only output rules.

Required feasibility constraints:

- no historical reads are enabled by this milestone;
- no UI, API, dashboard, or Route Watch behavior changes are introduced;
- the adapter remains the only consumer-facing translation layer;
- phrases are selected only from the approved catalog;
- prohibited language is suppressed before display;
- low evidence suppresses historical language or uses an approved caveat;
- no raw counts, timestamps, time windows, confidence percentages, source metadata, or reliability scoring are exposed;
- historical language remains secondary to current awareness.

The adapter should not support clearance, duration, time-window, count, recency, scoring, reliability, or route-advice language.
