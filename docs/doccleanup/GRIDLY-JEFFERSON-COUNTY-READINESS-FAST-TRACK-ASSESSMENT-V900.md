# GRIDLY Jefferson County Readiness Fast-Track Assessment V900

## 1. Executive Summary

V900 is a documentation-only fast-track assessment of Jefferson County as a potential future county candidate for the Gridly County Platform Foundation. The assessment applies the V550 County Readiness Fast-Track Framework to determine whether Jefferson County should be treated as a validated future county candidate for governed planning.

Jefferson County provides a Southeast Texas expansion profile with substantial coastal, industrial, port, evacuation, hurricane, urban, suburban, rural-edge, and interstate-corridor complexity. Its planning value is strongest where the platform needs to evaluate readiness for a county with major population centers, petrochemical and port-adjacent movement, I-10 regional travel, U.S. 69 / U.S. 96 / U.S. 287 corridor convergence, Sabine-Neches-area context, coastal and marsh-adjacent geography, and multi-community awareness needs.

Scope is limited to assessment only. V900 does not activate Jefferson County, onboard Jefferson County, create Jefferson County packages, create registry entries, create storage namespaces, create fixtures, create migrations, modify runtime behavior, modify Supabase, enable historical reads, enable history UI, enable historical APIs, resume DriveTexas, enable Transportation Intelligence, or authorize implementation.

Final determination preview: **VALIDATED COUNTY CANDIDATE WITH OBSERVATIONS**. Jefferson County appears suitable for future governed planning, but future work must explicitly validate authoritative boundaries, coastal and water-adjacent containment, industrial and port-adjacent corridor segmentation, municipal and unincorporated community structures, source availability, and County Platform Foundation compatibility before any implementation milestone is considered.
