# GRIDLY Polk County Readiness Fast-Track Assessment V800

## 1. Executive Summary

V800 is a documentation-only fast-track assessment of Polk County as a potential future county candidate for the Gridly County Platform Foundation. The assessment applies the V550 County Readiness Fast-Track Framework to determine whether Polk County should be treated as a validated future county candidate for governed planning.

Polk County provides an East Texas expansion profile that is regionally connected to the Liberty, Montgomery, Chambers, and San Jacinto candidate sequence while introducing a broader rural-community, lake-adjacent, forested, and highway-corridor context. Its planning value is strongest where the platform needs to evaluate county readiness beyond immediate Liberty adjacency, with attention to U.S. 59 / I-69 movement, Livingston-area awareness, Lake Livingston influence, rural communities, county-edge containment, and regional service patterns.

Scope is limited to assessment only. V800 does not activate Polk County, onboard Polk County, create Polk County packages, create registry entries, create storage namespaces, create fixtures, create migrations, modify runtime behavior, modify Supabase, enable historical reads, enable history UI, enable historical APIs, resume DriveTexas, enable Transportation Intelligence, or authorize implementation.

Final determination preview: **VALIDATED COUNTY CANDIDATE WITH OBSERVATIONS**. Polk County appears suitable for future governed planning, but future work must explicitly validate authoritative boundaries, rural and lake-adjacent community structures, corridor segmentation, source availability, and County Platform Foundation containment before any implementation milestone is considered.

## 2. Framework Basis

This assessment is based on the following prior milestones and lessons:

- **V459 through V475 County Platform Foundation.** V800 preserves activation architecture, Liberty normalization, county registry contract, storage namespace discipline, read/write containment, package fixture standards, readiness review, governance review, dry-run expectations, evidence lifecycle, execution discipline, and consolidation requirements.
- **V500 through V507 Montgomery County Readiness Program.** V800 reuses the Montgomery program's lessons about adjacency, regional movement, cross-county containment, county-package planning, evidence-first readiness, governance-first onboarding, and protected-boundary discipline.
- **V600 Chambers County Readiness Fast-Track Assessment.** V800 carries forward the lesson that fast-track validation may record strong county-candidate value while preserving observations around specialized geography, corridor behavior, and containment.
- **V700 San Jacinto County Readiness Fast-Track Assessment.** V800 builds on the San Jacinto rural-county pattern by evaluating a larger East Texas candidate with similar rural, lake-adjacent, and corridor-aware considerations, without treating prior candidate validation as activation authority.

## 3. Candidate Suitability

- Score: **HIGH READINESS**
- Strategic value:
  - Polk County is a plausible future county candidate because it extends the county platform's planning lens farther into East Texas while remaining regionally coherent with the Liberty, Montgomery, Chambers, and San Jacinto assessment sequence.
  - It provides useful learning around rural communities, lake-adjacent awareness, forested areas, regional highway movement, and county service centers.
  - It can be evaluated without implying county activation, onboarding, package creation, registry creation, storage provisioning, fixture creation, runtime behavior, Supabase changes, historical-read exposure, DriveTexas resumption, or Transportation Intelligence activation.
- Regional value:
  - Polk County has meaningful regional movement relevance through north-south highway travel, connections toward San Jacinto County and Liberty County, and East Texas service patterns.
  - The county adds a broader expansion-learning case beyond direct Liberty adjacency while still fitting the overall county-platform foundation narrative.
- Expansion value:
  - Polk County can help test whether the fast-track framework remains useful for a county that is rural and corridor-connected but less directly tied to the initial Liberty-centered operating footprint than Chambers or San Jacinto.
  - It provides a candidate profile for later evaluating larger rural-county geography, lake-area reporting, dispersed communities, and cross-county travel without activating operational routing.
- Observations:
  - Candidate suitability is strong, but Polk County should remain a planning candidate only until future evidence, package, governance, dry-run, registry, storage, fixture, and activation gates are separately satisfied.
  - The county's regional value is partly indirect; future prioritization should not assume that candidate validation creates onboarding priority.
- Risks:
  - Polk County's broader East Texas position could be overstated as immediate platform expansion readiness if the candidate label is not carefully bounded.
  - Regional highway relevance could create pressure to discuss operational transportation behavior, which is not authorized by this assessment.
- Future gates:
  - Future candidate planning must pass County Platform Foundation evidence, containment, package, registry, storage, governance, dry-run, rollback, execution, and activation gates before any implementation work.

- Geographic and county-package readiness are high at a planning level because future boundary-based containment and package identity appear feasible.

- It is appropriate as a future East Texas rural, lake-adjacent, and highway-corridor candidate when the platform needs to evaluate broader regional expansion learning without creating activation pressure.

- Require containment tests for county-edge roads, lake-area references, and community labels before dry-run review.

Executive recommendation: **Maintain Polk County as a validated future county candidate with observations. Do not activate, onboard, implement, package, register, provision, migrate, expose, or operate Polk County until a later milestone grants explicit authority.**
